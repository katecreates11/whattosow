# What To Sow — Nano Banana 2 Prompt Library

## How to Use These Prompts

1. Open Gemini (gemini.google.com) or Google AI Studio
2. Paste the prompt exactly as written
3. If the result is 80% right, iterate conversationally: "make the green darker", "move the shed to the left", "add more negative space on the right"
4. Once you get a result you love, use it as a reference image for the next prompt to maintain consistency
5. For each batch, upload your first successful image and say: "Generate the next image in exactly this style" before pasting the new subject prompt

## Workflow for Consistency

**Start with the hero image. Get that right first. Then use it as the style anchor for everything else.**

After generating the hero, upload it as a reference and prepend each subsequent prompt with:
> "Match the exact illustration style, colour palette, line quality, and texture of the uploaded reference image."

## PRIORITY 1: Homepage Hero Illustration

This goes in the right column of your homepage hero section (the space already reserved for commissioned art). Needs clear space on the left for your headline text.

### Prompt:

```
Create a flat hand-drawn editorial illustration for a UK gardening website homepage.

The scene: a small wooden allotment shed with its door open, seen from the front. Around it, raised beds overflow with simplified vegetable forms — broad bean pods, climbing runner beans on poles, fat courgettes, carrot tops poking from soil. A garden fork leans against the shed. The composition is weighted to the right side, leaving generous empty space on the left third for text overlay.

Style: warm and handmade. Textured dry-media linework (like crayon or coloured pencil on paper) in a single muted sage green, with selective flat colour fills in dusty blush pink and golden ochre — maximum 3-4 colours total. Visible paper grain texture throughout. Warm cream parchment background. All forms are simplified to essential shapes — not realistic, but immediately recognisable.

Completely flat — no shadows, no gradients, no perspective, no 3D rendering. No text. No people. Mood: cheerful and abundant, like a Saturday morning at the allotment in June.
```

### Alternate hero (Mode C — flat silhouettes, more minimal):

```
Create a minimal flat illustration of an allotment garden scene for a website hero image.

Layered silhouette shapes in muted sage greens (light to dark), dusty blush pink, and warm ochre on a cream parchment-textured background. A small shed silhouette in the middle distance. Foreground: simplified vegetable plant shapes (bean poles, leafy tops, climbing tendrils) as flat colour blocks with no outlines. Background: gentle rolling hill shapes suggesting garden beds.

Composition weighted to the right, with clear empty space on the left third for headline text. No outlines, no shadows, no gradients, no perspective. Forms defined only by colour contrast. Extremely simplified — shapes should read as botanical but approach abstraction. Visible paper grain texture. Warm, optimistic, quiet. 16:9 aspect ratio.
```

## ITERATION CHEAT SHEET

Once you have a result, use these conversational tweaks:

**Colour adjustments:**
- "Shift the greens cooler / warmer"
- "Make the background more cream, less white"
- "Reduce the saturation by 20%"
- "Use only two colours: sage green and warm cream"

**Composition:**
- "More negative space on the left"
- "Pull the subject smaller, more breathing room"
- "Crop tighter on the shed"

**Style adjustments:**
- "Make the linework more textured, like crayon on rough paper"
- "Remove all outlines, use only flat colour shapes"
- "Make it more minimal — half as many elements"
- "More hand-drawn, less digital-looking"

**Common fixes:**
- "Remove all shadows and depth"
- "Make it completely flat — no 3D effect"
- "Remove the text" (NB2 sometimes adds text unprompted)
- "Warmer background — like aged paper"
