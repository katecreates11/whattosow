**ONE TIP**

Check your soil temperature before sowing outdoors. Most seeds need soil above 7°C to germinate — below that, they just sit and rot. A cheap soil thermometer (£3-5) saves a lot of wasted seed. Our tool shows your current soil temperature when you enter your postcode.

## Month-by-month crop suggestions

Use these as starting points. Check the site for the current list.

### January
- Plan your plot, order seeds
- Sow: broad beans (indoors), chillies, peppers (on heat mat)
- Chit seed potatoes from late Jan

### February
- Sow indoors: tomatoes, peppers, aubergines, chillies
- Sow outdoors (mild areas): broad beans, peas, parsnips
- Tip: Don't rush — cold soil kills more seedlings than late sowing

### March
- Peak indoor sowing month: tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, courgettes
- Outdoor: broad beans, peas, lettuce, radishes, carrots, beetroot, spinach
- Tip: Soil thermometer. 7°C minimum for most seeds

### April
- Harden off indoor sowings
- Direct sow: most hardy crops, French beans (late April)
- Sow indoors: runner beans, squash, pumpkins, sweetcorn
- Tip: Last frost is coming — watch the forecast

### May
- Plant out after last frost: tomatoes, courgettes, squash, peppers
- Direct sow: runner beans, French beans, sweetcorn
- Successional sowing: lettuce, radishes, spring onions
- Tip: Fleece ready for late frosts

### June
- Everything goes in now. Plant out remaining tender crops
- Sow: autumn/winter brassicas (kale, Brussels sprouts, cabbage)
- Keep succession sowing salads
- Tip: Water in the evening to reduce evaporation

### July
- Sow: spring onions, lettuce, pak choi, rocket, beetroot (for autumn)
- Sow: kale, Swiss chard for winter
- Tip: Don't let courgettes get too big — pick at 15-20cm

### August
- Sow: spring cabbage, winter lettuce, Japanese onion sets
- Green manure on empty beds (phacelia, field beans)
- Tip: This is the month people forget to sow. Don't stop

### September
- Sow: broad beans (overwintering), garlic
- Plant: onion sets (overwintering varieties)
- Green manure everywhere you've harvested
- Tip: Autumn sowing = earlier spring harvest

### October
- Sow: broad beans, garlic, overwintering onion sets
- Clear spent crops, add compost
- Tip: Every bed should be covered — crops, mulch, or green manure

### November
- Last chance for garlic if ground isn't frozen
- Tidy, compost, plan next year
- Tip: Time to browse seed catalogues

### December
- Rest. Plan. Order seeds early for best selection
- Tip: The best thing you can do for next year's plot is a good plan
