**Preview text:** Last frost date isn't a guess — here's how to read it.

---

Hi,

Quick one today — if you've ever wondered what "last frost date" actually means and why the tool keeps banging on about it, here's the short version.

Your last frost date is the estimated date after which you're unlikely to get a ground frost. For most of England, that's somewhere between mid-April and mid-May. Up in Scotland or at altitude, it can be late May or even June. Down in Cornwall, some folk are frost-free by late March.

Why does it matter? Because almost every sowing window — indoors and outdoors — is anchored to this date. "Sow indoors 6 weeks before last frost" is useless if you don't know when your last frost actually is. That's what the tool calculates for you: it takes your postcode, estimates your frost date, and shifts every single sowing recommendation accordingly.

So when the site says "sow broad beans outdoors from 1 March" for your area, that's not a guess. It's tied to your local conditions.

You can explore how frost dates vary across the whole UK here — there's a 66-day gap between the Isles of Scilly and Shetland:

https://whattosow.co.uk/frost-map

Have a look. It's quite something when you see it mapped out.

Hi,

A week in — hope you've had a chance to poke around the tool. Here's what's popular with growers right now, in case you need a nudge.

**Broad beans** — If you haven't got these in yet, now's the time. Aquadulce Claudia is the classic overwinter variety, but for a March sowing Super Aquadulce or Crimson Flowered both do well. Direct sow, no fuss.

**Onion sets** — Sets are more reliable than seed at this point in the year. Push them in so the tip just shows. Sturon and Stuttgarter are bulletproof. Red Baron if you want something for salads.

**Peas** — Early varieties like Kelvedon Wonder can go out now under a cloche or in a sheltered spot. Or start them in guttering indoors and slide the whole lot out in a few weeks.

**Chillies** — The one people forget. If you want chillies by summer, the sowing window is closing. They need a long season. Get them on a warm windowsill this week if you haven't already.
